These Three Days In History: April 5 - 8, 1998

April 6: Teletubbies premieres on PBS in the US.

April 7: Warhammer: Dark Omen (article here) premieres on the PlayStation in North America and Europe.

April 7: Citicorp and Travelers Group announce their plans to merge into Citigroup for $140 billion ($207.3 billion in 2016 money).

April 7: Cardcaptor Sakura premieres on NHK in Japan. (TW: bright, flashing lights and images) The edited English version, Cardcaptors, would be released on North American shores on June 17, 2000, starting with the eighth episode.

April 7: (TW: flashing lights) The Goo Goo Dolls' single "Iris" debuts, as part of the soundtrack of the Nicolas Cage-starred movie City of Angels.

April 7: (TW: partial nudity, explosions) Overnight Delivery premieres on video, featuring Paul Rudd, Reese Witherspoon, Christine Taylor, Sarah Silverman, and Tobin Bell.
