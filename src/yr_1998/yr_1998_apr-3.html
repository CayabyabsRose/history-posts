This Week In History: April 9 - 15, 1998

April 9: American actress Elle Fanning is born.

April 9: (TW: murder, smoking, nudity) Shadow of Doubt premieres in theatres, featuring Melanie Griffith, Tom Berenger, Huey Lewis, and John Ritter.

April 9: The Price Is Right broadcasts its 5000th episode on CBS, where every pricing game's prize in that episode is a brand new car. Also, the studio where the game show is usually filmed is renamed the Bob Barker Studio.

April 10: The Good Friday Agreement, or the Belfast Agreement, is signed between the Irish and British governments, with disagreements from the Democratic Unionists and a few Northern Ireland parties.

April 10: Premiering in theatres this week are:
(TW: medical procedures, flashing lights) The remake of Wim Wenders's film Wings of Desire, City of Angels (dir. Brad Silberling), featuring Nicolas Cage, Meg Ryan, Andre Braugher, and Colm Feore.
(TW: car collisions, ableist slur, smoking) My Giant (dir. Michael Lehmann), featuring Billy Crystal, Dan Castellaneta, Steven Seagal, Doris Roberts, Rider Strong, and Gheorghe Muresan.
(TW: explosions) The Odd Couple II (dir. Howard Deutch), featuring Jack Lemmon as Felix, and Walter Matthau as Oscar.
(TW: partial nudity, shootings, body horror, flashing lights and images) Species II (dir. Peter Medak), featurng Natasha Henstridge, Michael Madsen, Marg Helgenberger, James Cromwell, Mykelti Williamson, George Dzundza, and Peter Boyle.
(TW: flashing images) 3 Ninjas: High Noon at Mega Mountain, featuring Hulk Hogan, Jim Varney, and Victor Wong.
(TW: smoking) The Big One, a documentary from Michael Moore.

April 11: The CableACE Awards are discontinued because the Emmy Awards now recognize cable programming.

April 12: (TW: natural disasters) An earthquake happens near Bovec, in Slovenia, in the Krn Mountains, measuring 5.7 on the Richter scale.
