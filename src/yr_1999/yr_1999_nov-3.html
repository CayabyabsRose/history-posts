This Week In History: November 11 - 17, 1999

November 11: American voice actress Mary Kay Bergman, which her credits include Dr. Blight in Captain Planet and the Planeteers, Beauty and the Beast, The Fairly Oddparents, South Park, and many others, passes away.

November 11: The following video games premiere:
(TW: gore in commercial) ChuChu Rocket! (commercial here, article here) for the Dreamcast in Japan, later released to the Game Boy Advance in Japan on March 21, 2001.
Giga Wing (article here) for the Sega Dreamcast in Japan.
Garou: The Mark of the Wolf for Japanese arcades. (article only)

November 11: This day would be the last upside-down date until January 1, 6000.



November 12: (TW: insects, body horror, ableist slur) Courage the Cowardly Dog (I remember watching this one!), and Mike, Lu, and Og premiere on Cartoon Network.

November 12: Céline Dion's album All The Way... A Decade of Song is released, featuring "That's The Way It Is", and "If Walls Could Talk".

November 12: (TW: natural disasters) Another earthquake hits Turkey in the city of Düzce in Düzce Province, leaving 845 dead, 4,948 injured, 55,000 citizens displaced, and around $1 billion ($1.5 billion in 2017 money) in damages.

November 12: President Clinton signs the Gramm-Leach-Billey Act (BOOOOO!), repealing part of the Glass-Steagall Act, further deregulating big corporations and banks.

November 12: (TW: shootings, explosions, natural disasters, bright lights) Judgment Day premieres on video, featuring Mario Van Peebles, Ice-T, Suzy Amis, and Coolio.
