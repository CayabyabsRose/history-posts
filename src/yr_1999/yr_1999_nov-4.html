This Week In History: November 18 - 24, 1999

November 18: (TW: death) The Aggie Bonfire collapses during construction in Texas A&M, killing 12 students, and injuring 27.



November 18: (TW: bright, flashing lights, nudity) Chrono Cross (article here), the successor to Chrono Trigger is released for the PlayStation in Japan.



Also released is:
The debut of Alundra 2: A New Legend Begins (Alundra 2: The Mystery of Magic Evolution) (article here) for the PlayStation in Japan.

November 18 - 19: In Istanbul, Turkey, the OSCE summit takes place, where a verbal debate ensues between Russia and the West over the Second Chechen War, and NATO intervention in Kosovo, along with the signing of the Charter for European Security, and the Istanbul Declaration.

November 19: Shenzhou 1, China's first unmanned spacecraft, has lifted off.

November 19: (TW: flashing lights) John Carpenter (not the director, but the IRS agent) becomes the first contestant to win $1 million on the US edition of Who Wants To Be A Millionaire?



November 19: (TW: bright lights, facial horror, ableist slur) The Longest Journey (article here) premieres on PCs in Norway, later to the UK on April 20, 2000.

Games also premiering are:
(TW: insects, gore, shootings, flashing lights, war scenes) Half-Life: Opposing Force (trailer here, article here), the expansion pack to Half-Life, premieres on PCs in North America.
Mobil 1 Rally Championship in Europe for PCs. (article only)

November 21: Pokémon Gold and Pokémon Silver (commercial here) premiere for the Game Boy Color in Japan.

November 21: Elizabeth Brotons Rodriguez and her son, Elián González, with 12 other people, leave Cuba on a small aluminum boat, where Rodriguez and ten others die during the crossing. González and two others float at sea until fishermen spot them and hand the three survivors over to the U.S. Coast Guard.



November 22: Hercules: The Legendary Journeys, which premiered on January 16, 1995, ends its run.

November 22: After Immigration and Naturalization Services release Elián González to his great-uncle, González's father notifies Elián's great-uncle, Lazáro González, that González and his mother have left Cuba. Lazáro, along with other Cuban Americans, decide that Elián should stay in Miami.
