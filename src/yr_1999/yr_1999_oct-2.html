This Week In History: October 7 - 13, 1999



October 7: The anime adaptation of Excel Saga (article here), based on Rikdo Koshi's manga, premieres on TV Tokyo in Japan.

October 7: House Hunters premieres on HGTV. (article only)

October 7: The video game adaptation of Xena: Warrior Princess (article here) premieres on the PlayStation.

October 7: The Hi-Line premieres at the Austin Film Festival.

October 7: (TW: terrorism, bombings, explosions) Final Voyage premieres, featuring Dylan Walsh, Ice-T, and Erika Eleniak, and (TW: ableist slur) the documentary My Best Fiend (dir. Werner Herzog).

October 8: Trouble Chocolate premieres on TV Asahi in Japan. (article only)

October 8: Premiering in theatres this week are:
(TW: transphobia, smoking, alcohol, shootings) Boys Don't Cry (dir. Kimberly Peirce), featuring Hilary Swank, Chloë Sevigny, and Peter Saarsgard.
(TW: plane collisions mention, sexual scenes) Random Hearts (dir. Sydney Pollack), featuring Harrison Ford, Kristin Scott Thomas, Charles S. Dutton, Bonnie Hunt, Dennis Haysbert, Richard Jenkins, Paul Guilfoyle, Lynne Thigpen, and Edie Falco.
(TW: ableist slur) Superstar (dir. Bruce McCulloch), featuring Molly Shannon, Will Ferrell, Harland Williams, Elaine Hendrix, and Glynis Johns.
(TW: ableist slur, smoking, stalking, sexual scenes, sexist slur) The Woman Chaser, featuring Patrick Warburton.
(TW: smoking, shootings, car collisions, flashing images) The Limey (dir. Steven Soderbergh), featuring Terence Stamp, Leslie Ann Warren, Luis Guzmán, and Peter Fonda.
The Canadian release of Jacob Two Two Meets the Hooded Fang, featuring Gary Busey, Miranda Richardson, Ice-T, Maury Chaykin, and Max Morrow.
The Telluride Film Festival release of Held Up.

October 9: NASA's SR-71 "Blackbird" aircraft has its last flight before being retired.

October 9: TV movie Don't Look Under the Bed premieres on the Disney Channel.

October 9: Kiss the Sky premieres in theatres, featuring William Petersen, Gary Cole, Sheryl Lee, and Terence Stamp. (article only)

October 9: "Heartbreaker" by Mariah Carey & Jay Z is now on the top of the Billboard Hot 100 charts. The only song peaking this week is:
(TW: alcohol) "Angels Would Fall" by Melissa Etheridge at #51.



October 9 - 11: The first Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival is held in Coachella, CA, USA.

October 10: Blue Gender premieres on TBS in Japan.



October 10: The London Eye, or the Millennium Wheel is lifted into place in the South Bank of the Thames in London, England, UK.

October 11: Shooting begins on THE LORD OF THE RINGS film trilogy.



October 11: (TW: bright, flashing lights, insects, explosions) Rare releases Jet Force Gemini (gameplay here, article here) on the Nintendo 64 in North America, later released to Europe on November 2, 1999.

October 12: The six billionth-human is born in Sarajevo, Bosnia-Herzegovina.



October 12: Pac-Man World (gamepley here, article here) premieres on the PlayStation in North America.



October 12: Harlem Globetrotter, and NBA center whose career included playing for the San Francisco/Philadelphia Warriors, the 76ers, and the Lakers, Wilt Chamberlain, passes away.

October 12: (TW: bright, flashing lights, ableist slur) Ricky Martin's "Shake Your Bon-Bon" premieres as a single.

October 13: The US Senate rejects the ratification (51-48) of the Comprehensive Nuclear-Test Ban Treaty, or the CTBT.

October 13: (TW: spinning images) The Big O premieres on WOWOW in Japan.

October 13: Bridge of Dragons has its US release, featuring Dolph Lundgren, Cary-Hiroyuki Tagawa, along with the premiere of The Story of Us. (article only)
