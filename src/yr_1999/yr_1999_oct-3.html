This Week In History: October 14 - 20, 1999



October 14: Square premieres Threads of Fate (Dewprism) (commercial here, article here) on the PlayStation in Japan.

Also released are:
Speed Devils (article here) for the Dreamcast in North America, later to Japan on November 18, 1999.
Robbit Mon Dieu (Jumping Flash! 3) for the PlayStation in Japan. (article only)

October 14: Now and Then, Here and There premieres on WOWOW in Japan.

October 15: American actress Bailey Madison is born.

October 15: A National Geographic press conference announces the discovery of an Archaeoraptor from China, which later turned out to be a forgery made up from several real fossils.



October 15: (TW: assault, smoking, gore, explosions, shootings, car collisions) The film adaptation of Chuck Palahniuk's novel Fight Club (dir. David Fincher), premieres widely in theatres, featuring Edward Norton, Brad Pitt, Helena Bonham Carter, Meat Loaf, and Jared Leto.

Also premiering in theatres this week are:
The wide release of The Story of Us (dir. Rob Reiner), featuring Bruce Willis, Michelle Pfeiffer, Rita Wilson, Rob Reiner, Julie Hagerty, Tim Matheson, Red Buttons, and Betty White.
The limited release of The Straight Story (dir. David Lynch), featuring Richard Farnsworth, Sissy Spacek, and Harry Dean Stanton.
The Hong Kong releases of A Beautiful New World (article only), and (TW: smoking, shootings, gore, bombings) Century of the Dragon.
The US release of When Love Comes Along. (article only) (TW: mention of abuse in article) The wide release of >Julien Donkey-Boy (dir. Harmony Korine, screenwriter for Kids), featuring Ewen Bremner, Chloë Sevigny, and Werner Herzog. (article only)
(TW: riots, bombings) The Omega Code, featuring Casper van Dien, Michael York, and Michael Ironside.
Gregory's Two Girls (dir. Bill Forsyth), featuring John Gordon Sinclair. (article only)





October 16: The Amanda Show and 100 Deeds for Eddie McDowd premiere on Nickelodeon.



October 16: The first anime adaptation of Hunter × Hunter, based on Yoshihiro Togashi's manga, premieres on Fuji TV in Japan.

October 16: Dead or Alive 2 premieres in Japanese arcades. (article only)

October 16: American actor, radio and TV personality, and writer Jean Shepherd, or Shep (A Christmas Story, In God We Trust: All Others Pay Cash, The Ferrari in the Bedroom) passes away.

October 16: Excellent Cadavers premieres on HBO, featuring Chazz Palminteri, and F. Murray Abraham. (article only)

October 16: "Heartbreaker" by Mariah Carey & Jay Z is on its second week at the top of the Billboard Hot 100. Songs peaking this week are:
"Music of My Heart (from the film Music of the Heart)" by NSYNC & Gloria Estefan at #2.
(TW: flashing lights and images, car collisions) "If I Could Turn Back the Hands of Time" by R. Kelly at #12.
(TW: ableist slur, abuse mention) "Girls Best Friend" by Jay Z at #52.

October 17: Greek American physicist and mathematician Nicholas Metropolis passes away.

October 17: (TW: hijackings, explosions, plane collisions, terrorism) TV movie Thrill Seekers (The Time Shifters) premieres on TBS in the US, featuring Casper Van Dien, Catherine Bell, Theresa Saldana, and Martin Sheen.



October 18: Pokémon Yellow: Special Pikachu Edition (commercial here, article here) premieres on the Game Boy in North America.

October 18: The Messenger: The Story of Joan Arc has its US limited premiere, along with (TW: alcoholism, car collisions, implied rape, flashing lights) Body Shots, featuring Sean Patrick Flanery, Jerry O'Connell, Tara Reid, and Emily Procter.

October 19: Australian country singer Keith Urban releases his second self-titled album.

October 19: 98 Degrees release their album This Christmas, featuring "This Gift".

October 19: (TW: explosions) Children of the Corn 666: Isaac's Return premieres, along with (TW: nudity, smoking, flashing lights) Me and Will, and (TW: bombings, shootings, explosions, partial nudity, smoking) No Tomorrow (dir. Master P), featuring Master P, Pam Grier, and Gary Busey.



October 20: (TW: smoking) The (still running) anime adaptation of Eiichiro Oda's manga, One Piece (article here), premieres on Fuji TV in Japan.
